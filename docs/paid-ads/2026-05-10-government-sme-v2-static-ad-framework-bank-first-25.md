# Comparison One — Static Ad Framework Bank From “First 25” Examples

Source: Ian shared reference examples showing static ad frameworks that work for products.  
Use: adapt these into C1 finance ads without copying product-ad hype or fake social proof.  
Campaign: Government-backed zero-interest SME loan awareness.

## Why These Are Useful For C1

They are not lead-gen native, but the structures work because they create fast pattern recognition:

- familiar feed formats
- one clear idea per asset
- short mobile-first text
- a specific curiosity gap
- less “generic finance ad” energy

For C1, the move is to translate product frameworks into useful SME decision-support.

---

## Frameworks To Use

### 1. US vs Them

**C1 adaptation:** Default funding path vs funding-fit comparison.

**Overlay concept:**

```text
Applying blindly
vs
Checking the funding path first
```

**Support bullets:**

```text
One bank pathway
One rate to compare
May miss product fit
```

vs

```text
Program fit
Lender fit
Purpose + timing
```

**Use for:** broad cold audience, “don’t just apply to one bank” angle.

---

### 2. Claim

**C1 adaptation:** bold but supportable contrarian statement.

**Overlay options:**

```text
0% interest is not the whole story
```

```text
The lowest rate is not always the best path
```

```text
Your industry may fit. Your bank pathway may not.
```

**Use for:** stopping the scroll with a contradiction.

---

### 3. Lifestyle

**C1 adaptation:** practical SME owner context, not polished bank stock imagery.

**Visual:** workshop desk, stock boxes, supplier invoices, equipment, laptop.

**Overlay options:**

```text
Funding decisions happen before the application
```

```text
Cash flow. Equipment. Stock. Suppliers.
```

```text
Check the path before you apply
```

**Use for:** making the ad feel grounded and business-owner relevant.

---

### 4. Twitter / X-Style Post

**C1 adaptation:** thought-leader/commentary style, but no fake testimonials.

**Overlay/post concept:**

```text
Most SMEs hear “0% interest” and stop there.

But eligibility, purpose, timing and lender fit can matter more than the headline rate.
```

**Use for:** native-looking feed interruption. Keep it insight-led, not fake customer proof.

**Avoid:** fake named customers, fake verified accounts, fake engagement numbers.

---

### 5. Ugly

**C1 adaptation:** deliberately plain “owner note” creative. Looks less like an ad.

**Overlay concept:**

```text
If your business needs funding this month:

Don’t just ask “what’s the rate?”

Ask:
• Does the program fit?
• Does the lender fit?
• Does the product fit the purpose?
```

**Use for:** pattern interruption when polished fintech ads fatigue.

---

### 6. Offer

**C1 adaptation:** clear CTA card, one action.

**Overlay options:**

```text
Check your options in 90 seconds
```

```text
No credit pull
```

```text
Compare funding paths before applying
```

**Use for:** retargeting and direct-response feed placements.

---

### 7. Golden Nugget Review

**C1 adaptation:** insight/review-style quote without inventing a testimonial.

**Overlay concept:**

```text
“The rate matters.
But the wrong product can still be the wrong funding.”
```

**Support:**

```text
Purpose • Profile • Timing • Fit
```

**Use for:** value insight, founder-style creative, remarketing.

**Avoid:** fake review stars or fabricated customer names.

---

### 8. PR Callout

**C1 adaptation:** finance-news style announcement.

**Overlay options:**

```text
Australia’s new zero-interest SME loan program
```

```text
What supply-chain and manufacturing businesses should check first
```

```text
Not every business will fit the bank pathway
```

**Use for:** awareness-first cold ads.

**Avoid:** fake newspaper mastheads, fake government branding, official-looking seals.

---

### 9. iPhone Notes

**C1 adaptation:** simple Notes-app checklist.

**Overlay concept:**

```text
Before you apply for business funding:

1. What is the money for?
2. How fast do you need it?
3. Does the lender fit your profile?
4. Is the program actually available to you?
```

**Use for:** extremely readable native creative. Good for feed and Stories.

---

### 10. Feature Benefits

**C1 adaptation:** product feature → business owner benefit.

**Feature:** Compare government-backed programs.  
**Benefit:** Don’t assume the program fits before checking.

**Feature:** Compare business loans, credit lines, equipment finance, invoice finance.  
**Benefit:** See other pathways if one option does not fit.

**Feature:** No credit pull for the initial check.  
**Benefit:** Start without touching your credit file.

**Overlay concept:**

```text
One check.
Multiple funding paths.
No credit pull.
```

**Use for:** solution-aware and retargeting ads.

## Guardrails

- Do not use fake testimonials.
- Do not use fake screenshots from banks or government pages.
- Do not imply government affiliation.
- Do not use official-looking seals or logos.
- Do not claim everyone qualifies.
- Do not say “free money”.
- Do not put public compliance boilerplate on the creative.
- Keep each image to one idea only.
