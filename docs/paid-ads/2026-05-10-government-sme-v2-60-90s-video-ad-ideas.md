# Comparison One — 60–90s Video Ad Ideas

Campaign: Government SME awareness-first funding campaign  
Format preference: voiceover + animation/slideshow, not AI spokespeople except possibly the first 3-second hook  
Destination: `/advertorial/zero-interest-business-loans-australia` → `/quiz`  
Primary CTA: `Check what funding may fit` / `Compare funding options`  


## Production Direction

Use these as **voiceover-led explainer ads**, not talking-head ads.

Recommended style:

- Fast 3-second hook, optionally with a single AI spokesperson/news-reader frame.
- Immediately cut to kinetic text, animated cards, screenshots, simple diagrams, and business-context visuals.
- Voiceover carries the story.
- On-screen captions should be baked in.
- Use calm, credible finance-design style: deep green, gold CTA, white cards, simple icons, rate/criteria/checklist visuals.
- Avoid distressed borrower imagery. Show competent SME operators making a smarter funding decision.

## Priority 3-Second Hook Rules

The hook should do one job: make the viewer stop because the offer sounds unusually good.

Best hooks:

1. `Australia has government-backed zero-interest business loans. Most SME owners still have not checked them.`
2. `Zero-interest business loans are real — but not every business can access them.`
3. `If your business needs funding, check this before applying anywhere.`
4. `Even if you're in manufacturing, this government loan may not automatically fit.`
5. `One funding program won't fit every business. That's where lender matching matters.`

Optional AI spokesperson use:

- 0:00–0:03 only.
- Use a believable finance-news style frame, not influencer energy.
- Then cut away completely to animated explainer visuals.

## Idea 1 — “The Loan Most SME Owners Haven’t Checked”

### Best for
Cold awareness. This should be the first test because it educates people who have never heard about the program.

### Length
75–90 seconds.

### 3-second hook
`Australia has government-backed zero-interest business loans. Most SME owners still have not checked them.`

Visual:
- Optional 2.5s AI news-reader frame with headline banner.
- Smash cut to animated headline card: `Zero-interest business loans?`

### Story arc
1. Big awareness hook.
2. Explain the program simply.
3. Make it attractive.
4. Explain eligibility limits.
5. Pivot to C1 funding-fit check.

### Voiceover outline

0:00–0:03  
`Australia has government-backed zero-interest business loans — and many SME owners still haven't checked whether they fit.`

0:03–0:15  
`The program is designed to support eligible Australian businesses in specific supply-chain sectors, with zero interest on the loan itself.`

0:15–0:27  
`That sounds amazing. For the right business, it can be a serious opportunity: funding support without normal interest costs, at a time when cash flow matters.`

0:27–0:42  
`But there is a catch. Not every business can access it. Sector rules apply, applications can still go through bank assessment, and even manufacturing businesses do not automatically qualify.`

0:42–1:02  
`So if your business needs funding, the smart move is not to rely on one pathway. First, check the government program. Then compare what other lender and product options may fit your business.`

1:02–1:20  
`Comparison One helps Australian SME owners check what funding may fit their situation, then match with lender options based on the business need, timing, and profile.`

1:20–1:30  
`Start with a quick funding-fit check. `

### Visual structure

- Animated headline: `Zero-interest business loans are real`
- Simple card: `Government-backed support`
- Benefit cards: `No interest`, `SME support`, `Targeted sectors`
- Reality cards: `Sector rules`, `Bank assessment`, `Not automatic`
- Flow diagram: `Government program` + `Other funding pathways`
- C1 product match animation: working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, unsecured loans.
- CTA screen: `Check what funding may fit`

### Why it works
This gives the full education arc from zero awareness to action. It makes the offer feel attractive before introducing the limitation, which matches Ian’s correction.

## Idea 2 — “Sounds Too Good? Here’s the Catch”

### Best for
High-retention explainer ad. Strong for people who stop on the phrase “zero-interest”.

### Length
60–75 seconds.

### 3-second hook
`Zero-interest. Government-backed. Business funding. Sounds amazing — but here’s what to check first.`

Visual:
- Big kinetic text sequence: `ZERO INTEREST` → `GOVERNMENT-BACKED` → `BUSINESS LOANS`
- Quick record-scratch style transition into `Check the fine print`.

### Story arc
1. Make it feel like a rare opportunity.
2. Explain why people should care.
3. Introduce the catch without killing excitement.
4. Turn C1 into the practical next step.

### Voiceover outline

0:00–0:03  
`Zero-interest. Government-backed. Business funding. Sounds amazing — but here's what to check first.`

0:03–0:15  
`A government-backed no-interest loan can be one of the most attractive funding options an SME owner hears about.`

0:15–0:25  
`If your business fits the criteria, the appeal is obvious: useful capital, targeted support, and no interest cost on the loan.`

0:25–0:40  
`But these programs are not open to every business. They usually target specific industries, specific activities, and specific eligibility conditions.`

0:40–0:52  
`Even being in manufacturing may not be enough on its own. What matters is whether the business, purpose, timing, and lender assessment line up.`

0:52–1:08  
`That is why Comparison One starts with funding fit. If the government program is not the right path, our system helps match your business with lender options that may suit your actual funding need.`

1:08–1:15  
`Check what funding may fit. Comparison One. `

### Visual structure

- Glossy “opportunity” card with zero-interest headline.
- Animated checklist: `Sector`, `Purpose`, `Timing`, `Assessment`.
- Manufacturing icon appears, then zooms into sub-criteria: `Activity`, `Use of funds`, `Bank criteria`.
- C1 “match engine” visual: business need enters, lender/product cards appear.
- CTA card.

### Why it works
It preserves the “wow” factor while safely introducing limitations. The viewer does not feel like the ad is negative; it feels like a smart warning.

## Idea 3 — “Manufacturing Doesn’t Mean Automatic”

### Best for
Targeting manufacturing/logistics/trades operators. Good for a more qualified audience.

### Length
60–90 seconds.

### 3-second hook
`Manufacturing business? The zero-interest loan may still not be automatic.`

Visual:
- Workshop, packaging line, machinery silhouettes.
- Text overlay: `Manufacturing ≠ automatic eligibility`.

### Story arc
1. Speak to manufacturing without implying personal eligibility.
2. Explain the misconception.
3. Show the criteria layers.
4. Pivot to C1 matching.

### Voiceover outline

0:00–0:03  
`Manufacturing business? The zero-interest loan may still not be automatic.`

0:03–0:16  
`The government-backed zero-interest business loan has caught attention because it targets parts of Australia’s supply chain — including some manufacturing-related businesses.`

0:16–0:28  
`But being in manufacturing does not always mean the loan fits. Programs like this can depend on the exact business activity, what the funding is for, timing, and assessment criteria.`

0:28–0:45  
`A business making components, a business doing general repairs, and a business importing finished goods can all look different to a lender or program assessor.`

0:45–1:02  
`So before you build your funding plan around one program, check both paths: whether the government option may fit, and what lender options may work if it does not.`

1:02–1:18  
`Comparison One helps SME owners check what funding may fit their business and match with lender options across working capital, equipment, invoice finance, and unsecured business loans.`

1:18–1:30  
`Start with a funding-fit check. `

### Visual structure

- Animated manufacturing icons: factory, tools, stock, machine.
- Split examples: `Components`, `Repairs`, `Imports`, `Equipment purchase`.
- Criteria stack animation: `Industry` + `Use of funds` + `Timing` + `Lender assessment`.
- Two-path diagram: `Program may fit` / `Compare other funding`.
- C1 match screen.

### Why it works
It uses Ian’s specific point: even manufacturing may not automatically qualify. It is educational without being exclusionary.

## Idea 4 — “The Funding Fit Map”

### Best for
Explaining why C1 matters after the government-loan hook. This is the clearest product story.

### Length
75–90 seconds.

### 3-second hook
`One funding program cannot fit every business. Your funding path depends on what you need the money for.`

Visual:
- Animated map with a single road splitting into four funding pathways.

### Story arc
1. Start with the government program as the attention hook.
2. Explain why one product cannot fit all needs.
3. Map business needs to finance categories.
4. Position C1 as the matching system.

### Voiceover outline

0:00–0:03  
`One funding program cannot fit every business. Your funding path depends on what you need the money for.`

0:03–0:15  
`The new zero-interest government-backed loan is a serious opportunity for eligible SMEs. But it is still one type of funding, for specific situations.`

0:15–0:30  
`If you need cash for payroll or stock, that is a different problem from buying equipment. If you are waiting on unpaid invoices, that is different again.`

0:30–0:50  
`Working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, vehicle finance, unsecured business loans — each structure solves a different business problem.`

0:50–1:05  
`That is why Comparison One does not start with one lender. It starts with your funding need, timing, and business profile.`

1:05–1:22  
`If the government program may fit, check it. If it does not, or you need funding sooner, our system can help match you with lender options that may suit.`

1:22–1:30  
`Check what funding may fit. `

### Visual structure

- Funding map animation.
- Business needs become icons: payroll, supplier order, unpaid invoice, equipment, vehicle.
- Product cards slide in.
- Lender cards appear under each product category.
- C1 “fit check” form preview.

### Why it works
This makes C1’s mechanism clear. It also supports SEO/AEO positioning because it educates on funding categories, not just the government-news hook.

## Idea 5 — “Before You Apply Anywhere”

### Best for
Direct response. Most likely to drive quiz starts because the CTA is clear.

### Length
60–75 seconds.

### 3-second hook
`If your business needs funding, do this before applying anywhere.`

Visual:
- Fast slideshow: application form, bank logo blurred, government-program headline, C1 checklist.

### Story arc
1. Universal hook for funding-intent audience.
2. Introduce no-interest loan as the first thing to check.
3. Explain why applying blind can waste time.
4. Send to C1 funding-fit check.

### Voiceover outline

0:00–0:03  
`If your business needs funding, do this before applying anywhere.`

0:03–0:14  
`First, check whether the government-backed zero-interest business loan may apply to your situation. For eligible SMEs, it can be a very attractive option.`

0:14–0:28  
`But do not assume it fits automatically. Not every industry is covered, not every use of funds will line up, and even target-sector businesses may still face lender assessment.`

0:28–0:42  
`Second, compare what kind of funding your business actually needs. A working capital loan, invoice finance facility, or equipment finance product can each suit a different situation.`

0:42–0:58  
`Comparison One helps you check what funding may fit, then match with lender options based on your business need, timing, and profile.`

0:58–1:10  
`Before you apply blind, start with a funding-fit check. `

### Visual structure

- Animated checklist: `1. Check government option`, `2. Check funding need`, `3. Compare lender fit`.
- Timeline: `Apply blind` route fades out; `Check fit first` route brightens.
- Product cards with examples.
- CTA screen.

### Why it works
It is less editorial and more conversion-focused. The viewer gets a simple behavioural instruction: check fit before applying.

## Recommended Test Order

### Test first
**Idea 1 — The Loan Most SME Owners Haven’t Checked**  
Best all-rounder. Strong awareness hook, educational, and a clean C1 pivot.

### Test second
**Idea 2 — Sounds Too Good? Here’s the Catch**  
Best curiosity angle. Likely high retention if the hook lands.

### Test third
**Idea 5 — Before You Apply Anywhere**  
Most direct-response oriented. Good once the audience is warmer.

### Niche test
**Idea 3 — Manufacturing Doesn’t Mean Automatic**  
Use for manufacturing/logistics/trade audiences or retargeting.

### Product explainer
**Idea 4 — The Funding Fit Map**  
Best for retargeting and page visitors because it explains why C1 exists.

## Production Notes

### Visual asset types to prepare

- Animated headline cards.
- Government-program explainer card, with no official logos or crests.
- Criteria checklist animation.
- Product-category cards.
- Simple lender-match flow animation.
- C1 quiz preview.
- CTA end card.

### Voiceover style

- Calm Australian finance explainer.
- Not hypey, not desperate.
- Pace: 130–150 words per minute.
- Slight lift when explaining the zero-interest opportunity.
- Grounded tone when introducing the limits.


### Avoid

- `Guaranteed approval`
- `Everyone qualifies`
- `Free money`
- `Bad credit accepted`
- Official government logos or crests
- Making C1 look like it determines government-program eligibility
- AI spokesperson talking for the whole ad
