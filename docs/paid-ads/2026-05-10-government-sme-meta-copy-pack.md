# Meta Ads Copy Pack — Government SME Funding Access Gap

## Campaign Setup

- **Campaign:** Government SME Funding Access Gap — C1 Meta Launch
- **Destination:** `https://comparison-one-sanitypress.vercel.app/advertorial/zero-interest-loan-access-gap`
- **Objective:** Traffic → Advertorial engagement → Quiz start (primary conversion)
- **Primary CTA:** `Start my funding-fit check`
- **Alternate CTAs:** `Learn More`, `Compare options`, `Start my funding-fit check`, `Check my options`
- **Recommended audience mode:** Interest-based + broad (Australia-wide, SME owner/operator signals, no credit/income attribute targeting)
- **Exclusions:** Retargeting audiences (cold prospecting only); finance/credit targeting attributes
- **Compliance posture:** General information only. Comparison One is **not a lender** and **not a government agency**. No implied affiliation with the Economic Resilience Program. No approval guarantees. No personal hardship targeting.

## Rejected / Risky Copy

The following lines were considered and **rejected** for this campaign. Do not use under any circumstances without a specific compliance exception:

| Rejected copy | Reason | Risk level |
|---|---|---|
| "Can't qualify for the government loan? We can help." | Implies the reader is excluded from the program — personal failure framing. Breaches "no implied exclusion certainty" rule. | 🔴 High |
| "Rejected by the government scheme?" | Direct personal rejection framing. Combined with a funding solution implies Comparison One is an alternative to the government program. | 🔴 High |
| "Need money by Friday?" | Urgency exploitation / hardship bait. Meta policy violation. | 🔴 High |
| "Struggling with debt? Don't wait." | Personal financial hardship targeting — explicitly forbidden in campaign constraints. | 🔴 High |
| "Bad credit? Get approved." | Credit attribute targeting + implied approval guarantee. Double violation. | 🔴 High |
| "Zero-interest loophole." | Suggests gaming or exploiting the program. Undermines program integrity. | 🔴 High |
| "Banks don't want you to know this." | Conspiracy framing. Implies suppressed information. Breaches "no attacking banks" rule (Angle 3 constraint). | 🟠 Medium |
| "Guaranteed funding for Australian SMEs." | Absolute guarantee language — forbidden regardless of qualifiers. | 🔴 High |
| "The government program doesn't work — try this instead." | Direct attack on the government program. Breaches campaign thesis (inform, don't attack). | 🔴 High |
| "Approved today — no paperwork." | Guaranteed approval + speed bait. Misleading and non-compliant. | 🔴 High |
| "We work with the government to get you funded." | Implies government affiliation or endorsement. Comparison One is explicitly not a government agency. | 🔴 High |
| "All Australian businesses qualify for zero-interest loans." | False statement — program is sector-targeted. Also implies guaranteed program eligibility. | 🔴 High |
| "Skip the bank queue." | Implies Comparison One can bypass bank assessment. Misleading for a program that is bank-administered. | 🟠 Medium |

### Safe substitution examples

| Risky line | Safe alternative |
|---|---|
| "Can't qualify?" | "If the program may not fit your sector or timing..." |
| "Need money fast?" | "If timing matters alongside fit..." |
| "Struggling with cash flow?" | "Working capital finance may suit businesses with supplier or payroll cycles." |
| "Bad credit OK" | Not used; situational framing instead (subject to lender criteria). |
