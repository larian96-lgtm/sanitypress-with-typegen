## C1 Paid Ads Campaign Brief — Government SME Funding Access Gap

### Campaign Objective

- **Goal:** Drive qualified SME owners from Meta cold traffic into the Comparison One funding-fit check via an education-led advertorial that addresses the government zero-interest loan access gap.
- **Product/page:** Advertorial `/advertorial/zero-interest-loan-access-gap` → `/quiz` (funding-fit check / quote start).
- **Conversion action:** Quiz start / quote form start (primary). Advertorial CTA click (secondary).
- **Campaign type:** Problem-led — government program awareness triggering a funding-fit comparison.
- **Platform:** Meta Ads (first campaign for this product).
- **Geography:** Australia.
- **Budget:** Small first learning test — angle/creative testing priority, not scale.

### Audience Segments

**Segment 1 — Program-curious but unsure**

- **Situation:** Heard about the Economic Resilience Program zero-interest loans. Wants to know if their business qualifies. May be outside manufacturing/logistics/fuel/fertiliser/plastics sectors.
- **Trigger:** News coverage of government zero-interest loans creates a search moment.
- **Main objection:** "This sounds too good to be true — there must be a catch." / "If I don't qualify, I'm back to square one."
- **Best angle:** Angle 1 — Zero-interest does not mean zero decision.
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz`.

**Segment 2 — Eligible-ish but timing-pressured**

- **Situation:** Business may fit the sector criteria but needs funding faster than bank-administered assessment allows. Payroll, supplier, stock or job-mobilisation deadlines are immediate.
- **Trigger:** Cash-flow gap can't wait for program application windows and bank processing.
- **Main objection:** "Even if I qualify, the bank will take too long. I need the money now."
- **Best angle:** Angle 3 — Bank-administered bottleneck.
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz` (or direct to `/business-loans/working-capital` for retargeting).

**Segment 3 — Already delayed or declined by a bank**

- **Situation:** Applied, was delayed, declined, or couldn't provide the evidence the bank wanted. Now needs a realistic alternative.
- **Trigger:** Bank decline or stall on the program application. Feels stuck.
- **Main objection:** "If the bank said no, does any lender say yes?" / "I don't want to stack up more rejections."
- **Best angle:** Angle 4 — Wrong-start risk.
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz` or `/business-loans/knocked-back-by-bank`.

**Segment 4 — Needs different funding structure entirely**

- **Situation:** Business needs working capital, invoice finance, equipment/vehicle finance, trade finance, or an unsecured loan — not a term loan at any interest rate.
- **Trigger:** Owner is searching for "business loan" but hasn't identified the right product type for the problem.
- **Main objection:** "I need money. I don't know which product or lender is right."
- **Best angle:** Angle 2 — The access gap.
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz` (the funding-fit check is purpose-built for this).

### Angle Matrix

#### Angle 1 — Zero-interest does not mean zero decision

- **Hook:** "A zero-interest business loan still has to fit the business."
- **Core promise:** Before banking on one program, understand real eligibility, timing, and alternatives.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Advertorial content: program eligibility, bank-administered assessment, principal repayment obligation, sector exclusions.
  - business.gov.au: Economic Resilience Program criteria.
  - Comparison One existing pathways: working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, unsecured loans.
- **Best formats:** Advertorial (primary) / Image ad / Meta copy / Carousel (quiz steps).
- **Compliance notes:** No implied government affiliation. No "you won't qualify" certainty. General information only. Comparison One is not a lender or government agency.

#### Angle 2 — The access gap

- **Hook:** "Not every SME fits every government funding program."
- **Core promise:** If the program doesn't fit your sector, timing, or structure, compare the next realistic step.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Advertorial "who may be in the access gap" section.
  - Lender rate data from `/business-loans`.
  - C1 product pathways: working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, trade finance, unsecured loans.
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Meta copy / Video (15s explainer).
- **Compliance notes:** Avoid implying exclusion is certain — use "may not fit", "may be outside". No hardship framing.

#### Angle 3 — Bank-administered bottleneck

- **Hook:** "A government-backed loan can still involve bank assessment."
- **Core promise:** If timing matters, understand the pathway before applying blind.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Advertorial: "The bank-only bottleneck problem" section.
  - Comparison One bank-vs-non-bank guide.
  - Knocked-back-by-bank page.
- **Best formats:** Video (hook on timing pressure) / Image ad / Meta copy.
- **Compliance notes:** Don't attack banks or the program. Frame as practical awareness, not criticism. No "banks are slow" claims — use "involves bank assessment which may affect timing".

#### Angle 4 — Wrong-start risk (C1 core mechanism)

- **Hook:** "The costly mistake may be starting with the wrong funding path."
- **Core promise:** Before comparing rates, compare the funding structure. Working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, or unsecured loan? Start with fit.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - C1 long-form copy strategy source of truth: wrong-start risk as core mechanism.
  - Existing `/business-loans` hub with product categories.
  - Comparison One funding-fit check logic.
- **Best formats:** Advertorial / Meta copy / Carousel (product comparison cards).
- **Compliance notes:** Avoid implying Comparison One can fix every wrong start. Use "may", "could". General information only.

#### Angle 5 — Retargeting: Compare alternatives by need

- **Hook:** "Still comparing business funding options?"
- **Core promise:** Compare product types, not just lender names. See which category fits your real need.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Quiz landing page.
  - `/business-loans/working-capital`, `/business-loans/invoice-finance`, `/business-loans/equipment-finance`.
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Carousel (need-based cards).
- **Compliance notes:** Standard C1 compliance. No implied approval.

- `utm_content=bank_bottleneck_video_v1`
- `utm_content=wrong_start_carousel_v1`
- `utm_content=retarget_need_card_v1`

**Events:**
- Primary: Quiz start / quote form start (Meta pixel / CAPI event).
- Secondary: Advertorial page view, CTA click from advertorial, business-loans page click, time on page / scroll depth (if available).

**First test budget split:**
- Assuming small daily test budget:
  - 40% on Angle 1 (zero-interest ≠ zero decision) — lowest compliance risk, strongest advertorial alignment.
  - 25% on Angle 2 (access gap) — directly names the problem.
  - 20% on Angle 3 (bank bottleneck) — connects to C1 positioning.
  - 15% on Angle 4 (wrong-start risk) — evergreen C1 mechanism, test for future scaling.
  - Within each angle: 60% static image / 30% video / 10% carousel.

**Kill/scale rules:**
- Kill any angle after 2000 impressions if CTR < 0.3%.
- Kill any creative after 500 impressions if CTR < 0.2%.
- Scale angle to 2x budget when:
  - CTR > 0.8% (cold).
  - Advertorial bounce rate < 50%.
  - CTA click rate > 3%.
  - Quiz-start conversion rate > 1.5% of total ad clicks.
- Kill angle entirely if quiz-start cost exceeds 3x the median cost across all angles after 50 quiz events.
- Retargeting set: activate when advertorial visitors reach 500+ in 7 days.

### Critical Compliance Boundaries

- ✅ Comparison One is not a lender — state clearly.
- ✅ Comparison One is not a government agency — state clearly and prominently.
- ✅ General information only — include in all copy.
- ✅ No approval guarantees — never use "guaranteed", "bad credit accepted", "lowest rates", "funded today".
- ✅ No implied government affiliation — avoid language suggesting Comparison One is part of or endorsed by the Economic Resilience Program.
- ✅ No personal financial hardship targeting — do not call out personal hardship in Meta ad copy.
- ✅ No attacking the government program — frame as "check eligibility, compare alternatives", not "the program doesn't work".
- ❌ Avoid: "Can't qualify for the government loan?", "Rejected by the government scheme?", "Need money by Friday?", "Struggling with debt?", "Bad credit? Get approved.", "Zero-interest loophole.", "Banks don't want you to know this."
