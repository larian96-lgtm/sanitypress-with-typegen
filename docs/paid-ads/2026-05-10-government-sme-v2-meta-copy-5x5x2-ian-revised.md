## Copy Direction From Ian’s Revision

- Lead with eligibility and the new government-backed zero-interest business loan angle.
- Name relevant sectors early: supply chain, manufacturing, related industries.
- Make the practical uses concrete: cash flow, equipment, stock, supplier payments, growth.
- Explain the difference from the Covid SME lending scheme: access is currently tied to participating major banks.
- Use the bank criteria point as the tension: relevant industry does not automatically mean the program fits.
- Pivot to funding fit: purpose, business profile, timing, and criteria matter more than headline interest rate.
- CTA should be concrete: 90-second eligibility/funding-fit check, no credit pull for the initial check.
- No visible compliance boilerplate in public copy.

## 5 Primary Text Variants

### 1. Full / Longish Advertorial Copy

Australia has a government-backed zero-interest business loan program, and many SME owners still have not checked whether their business may fit.

If you operate in supply chain, manufacturing, or a related industry, it is worth paying attention. Funding may be available for business needs like cash flow support, equipment, stock, supplier payments, or growth plans.

But this is not the same as the SME lending scheme many business owners remember from Covid.

Access is currently tied to participating major banks. That means being in a relevant industry does not automatically mean the program is the right pathway. The bank’s criteria still matter. Your purpose for borrowing matters. Your business profile matters. Timing matters too.

And if the government-backed option does not fit, that does not always mean funding is off the table.

There is more to getting a business loan than the interest rate. A lower headline rate only helps if the product, lender, repayment structure, timing, and eligibility criteria actually match the business need.

Comparison One helps Australian SME owners check what funding may fit their situation. Compare pathways across government-backed programs, business loans, lines of credit, equipment finance, invoice finance, and other lender options.

Start with a quick 90-second eligibility and funding-fit check. No credit pull on your file for the initial check.
