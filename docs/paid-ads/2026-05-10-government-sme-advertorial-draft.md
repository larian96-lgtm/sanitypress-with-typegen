### What changed?

The Australian Government's Economic Resilience Program provides access to zero-interest loans for eligible businesses materially impacted by market disruptions linked to current conflicts.

According to official business.gov.au and National Reconstruction Fund Corporation information, the program is aimed at manufacturing and logistics businesses, particularly businesses in freight, fuel, fertiliser and plastics critical supply-chain sectors.

The program has two pathways:
- Bank-administered loans for businesses seeking up to $5 million and with turnover below the relevant threshold
- NRFC-administered loans for larger businesses or larger loan needs

Applications for the program opened on 20 April 2026.

That matters for businesses facing fuel and supply-chain disruption. But it does not mean every SME with cash-flow pressure will qualify.

### Why zero interest does not mean zero decision

A zero-interest loan still has to be repaid.

Official program information notes that while zero interest is payable, the principal must be repaid in full and standard bank fees may apply.

That means an owner still needs to ask:
- Can the business repay the principal?
- What fees apply?
- What term applies?
- Which bank is participating?
- What evidence is required?
- Does the business fit the eligible sector list?
- Does the disruption meet the program test?
- What happens if the business needs funds faster?
- What if the bank says no?

Zero interest can reduce cost, but it does not remove the need for funding fit.

---

### Who may be in the access gap?

Several groups may need a second pathway.

**Businesses outside the target sectors:**
A cafe dealing with wage pressure, a retailer waiting on stock revenue, a tradie needing a vehicle or a clinic funding fitout may have genuine cash-flow issues but not fit a manufacturing or logistics program.

**Businesses too small or too undocumented:**
Some SMEs may struggle to provide the evidence a bank wants quickly, even if the pressure is real.

**Businesses impacted indirectly:**
A business may be affected by higher fuel, freight or input costs but not directly fall into the target industry codes.

**Businesses that need funds faster than the process allows:**
Even a useful program can be too slow for payroll, stock, supplier or job-mobilisation deadlines.

**Businesses declined by the administering bank:**
A government program delivered through banks can still involve bank assessment. A decline should be understood before applying elsewhere.

**Businesses needing a different structure:**
A zero-interest loan may not fit if the business actually needs invoice finance, equipment finance, a line of credit, trade finance or a short-term working-capital facility.

---

### The bank-only bottleneck problem

Many business owners assume government-supported funding is automatically easier.

In practice, some programs are delivered through financial institutions, and those institutions still assess applications.

That can create a familiar bottleneck:
- The business is under timing pressure
- The owner hopes the program will solve it
- Eligibility or bank assessment takes time
- The business still needs to fund the week in front of it
- Plan B is unclear

This is not a reason to ignore the program. Eligible businesses should check it. It is a reason not to rely on one pathway without understanding alternatives.

### Do not turn a program disappointment into a bad loan

The emotional risk is real.

A business owner sees "zero interest", hopes it is the solution, then discovers they may not qualify or may need to wait. That disappointment can make the first available private funding offer feel more attractive than it should.

Pause before applying.

Ask:
- What exact gap am I solving?
- Is the cash needed for a short-term timing gap or a longer-term structural issue?
- Do I need a lump sum or flexible access?
- Do I have invoices, assets or contracts that support the application?
- Can the business afford repayments if the disruption lasts longer?
- Would advice from an accountant or finance specialist help?

---

### When to seek advice before borrowing

Seek qualified advice if:
- The business is behind on tax, super or wages
- Creditors are threatening action
- Directors may be personally exposed
- There is no clear repayment source
- The business is considering borrowing to cover ongoing losses
- The funding terms are hard to understand
- The loan involves security over property or important assets

The point is that SMEs need more than headlines.

- Whether they fit the program
- Whether the program fits the timing

That is what the funding-fit check is designed to clarify.

## FAQ

### What is the Economic Resilience Program?

It is an Australian Government program delivered through the National Reconstruction Fund framework that provides access to zero-interest loans for eligible businesses in targeted sectors affected by market disruptions.

### Who is it for?

Official information says it is aimed at manufacturing and logistics businesses, particularly freight, fuel, fertiliser and plastics critical supply-chain sectors. Eligibility should be checked on official government and NRFC pages.

### Is Comparison One part of the program?

No. Comparison One is not a government agency, participating bank or lender.

### Does zero interest mean free money?

No. The principal still has to be repaid and standard bank fees may apply under the bank-administered part of the program.

### What if my business does not qualify?

You may need to compare other funding pathways, such as working capital finance, invoice finance, equipment finance or unsecured business loans. Suitability and approval are not guaranteed.

### What if my bank declines the program loan?

Ask why the application was declined before applying elsewhere. The reason will help determine whether another lender type may fit.

### Is Comparison One a lender?

No. Comparison One is not a lender and does not make credit decisions. It helps Australian business owners compare possible funding pathways and move toward a realistic next step.

### Does Comparison One guarantee approval?

No. Approval, rates, terms, fees and timing depend on lender criteria and the business circumstances. The site provides general information only.
