## Meta Policy Check

### Personal attribute risk
- **Assessment:** ✅ Low
- No identity-based targeting ("Are you struggling?", "Bad credit?", "Rejected?")
- Situational language used throughout: "if your business may not fit", "if the program may not suit"
- Safe qualification: "check what funding may fit" — not "see if you qualify"

### Financial product risk
- **Assessment:** ✅ Low
- No interest rate claims, no "lowest rates", no "guaranteed approval"
- Public copy should use "may" / "could" naturally; avoid repeating boilerplate in every asset
- Affiliation/lender-status language should live in footer/legal or compliance notes, not every ad variant
- "Check what funding may fit" CTA does not imply approval determination
- "Check what funding you may qualify for" can be used sparingly when framed as a funding-fit check

### Landing-page consistency
- **Assessment:** ✅ Good
- V2 advertorial matches ad copy claims: awareness-first, education, reality check, then C1 pivot
- No broken summary text (new advertorial)
- All CTAs point to `/quiz` or external business.gov.au

### Recommended audience notes
- Interest-based + broad (Australia-wide, SME owner/operator signals)
- No credit/income/debt attribute targeting
- Exclude financial distress / debt management / credit repair interests
