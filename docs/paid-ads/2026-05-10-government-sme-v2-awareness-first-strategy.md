# C1 Paid Ads Campaign Brief V2 — Government SME Awareness-First Campaign

## Revision Note

**V2 changes based on Ian's correction:**

V1 assumed the audience already knew about the government no-interest-backed loan. V2 flips the flow:

1. **Educate first** — Announce the Australian Government no-interest SME loan as genuinely attractive news
2. **Make it seem amazing** — Explain the offer clearly: zero interest, government-backed, real program
3. **Reality check** — Not every business can access it; even manufacturing does not automatically qualify; eligibility depends on program rules, sector/activity, bank assessment, timing, and lender criteria
4. **Pivot** — If the program doesn't fit, Comparison One can run a funding-fit/qualification-style check and match the business with lenders/products that suit their needs

---

### Campaign Objective

- **Goal:** Drive qualified SME owners into awareness-first advertorial → C1 funding-fit match via Meta cold traffic
- **Product/page:** New advertorial `/advertorial/zero-interest-business-loans-australia` → `/quiz` (funding-fit check)
- **Conversion action:** Quiz start / quote form start (primary). Advertorial CTA click (secondary)
- **Campaign type:** News/awareness → education → reality check → alternative path
- **Platform:** Meta Ads first test
- **Geography:** Australia
- **Budget:** Small first learning test — angle/creative testing priority, not scale

### Audience Segments

**Segment 1 — Heard the news, wants to understand**

- **Situation:** Saw news about "government zero-interest loans" or "no-interest business funding." Isn't sure if it's real, who it's for, or how to access it. May be in or near the target sectors.
- **Trigger:** News coverage creates search/curiosity moment
- **Main objection:** "Is this real or just politics?" / "It sounds too good to be true"
- **Best angle:** Angle 1 — Awareness hook: Did you know?
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz`

**Segment 2 — In or near target sectors, wants to know if they qualify**

- **Situation:** Works in manufacturing, logistics, or supply-chain adjacent. Knows the program exists. Needs to understand exact eligibility rules, process, and timing.
- **Trigger:** Business faces cash-flow or disruption pressure
- **Main objection:** "Even if I'm in manufacturing, do I automatically qualify? How do I actually access this?"
- **Best angle:** Angle 2 — Amazing, but here's the reality
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz`

**Segment 3 — Outside target sectors, needs funding anyway**

- **Situation:** Runs a cafe, retail store, trades business, clinic, agency — outside manufacturing/logistics. The zero-interest program likely won't be available, but the funding need is real.
- **Trigger:** Cash-flow pressure, supplier deadline, equipment need, seasonal gap
- **Main objection:** "This doesn't apply to me — so what are my real options?"
- **Best angle:** Angle 3 — Even manufacturing doesn't auto-qualify (normalising it)
- **Suggested destination:** Advertorial → `/quiz`

**Segment 4 — Needs a lender match, not just a single product**

- **Situation:** Has a funding need but doesn't know which lender or product suits. Wants a system that matches them rather than applying blind.
- **Trigger:** Previous delay, decline, or confusion about where to start
- **Main objection:** "I don't want to apply to five places just to find one that works"
- **Best angle:** Angle 4 — Check what funding you may qualify for
- **Suggested destination:** `/quiz` (direct, cold test)

### Angle Matrix

#### Angle 1 — Awareness hook: Did you know?

- **Hook:** "Did you know the Australian Government backs zero-interest loans for some SMEs?"
- **Core promise:** What sounds too good to be true is actually real — for some businesses. Here's what you need to know.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Economic Resilience Program official info (business.gov.au)
  - Zero-interest, government-backed, bank-administered
  - Principal must be repaid; standard bank fees may apply
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Meta copy / Video (15s explainer)
- **Internal guardrail:** Avoid government-affiliation cues and approval guarantees. Keep legal boilerplate out of ad copy unless legally required.

#### Angle 2 — Amazing, but here's the reality

- **Hook:** "Zero-interest business loans sound incredible. Here's when they might not fit."
- **Core promise:** The offer is genuinely attractive, but eligibility, timing, sector rules, and bank assessment mean it's not available to everyone. Know the gap before you apply.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Program eligibility rules (sectors, turnover thresholds)
  - Bank-administered assessment — even government-backed loans go through lender review
  - C1 lender-matching service as alternative
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Meta copy / Video / Carousel
- **Internal guardrail:** Use "may not fit" / "may be outside criteria" without turning the ad into legal boilerplate.

#### Angle 3 — Even manufacturing doesn't auto-qualify

- **Hook:** "If you're in manufacturing, you still need to check — the zero-interest loan isn't guaranteed."
- **Core promise:** Even businesses in target sectors face bank assessment, documentation requirements, and timing constraints. No one is automatically approved.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - Program is sector-targeted but still involves lender assessment
  - Even eligible sectors must satisfy bank criteria, documentation, repayment capacity
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Meta copy / Video
- **Internal guardrail:** Avoid implying manufacturing businesses are excluded. No absolute statements.

#### Angle 4 — Check what funding you may qualify for (C1 match)

- **Hook:** "Need funding? Check what you may qualify for — lenders, not just a single program."
- **Core promise:** Instead of pinning hopes on one government program, use C1's system to match with lenders and products that fit your needs.
- **Proof/source basis:**
  - C1 funding-fit check (quiz)
  - C1 lender panel covering working capital, invoice finance, equipment finance, unsecured loans
- **Best formats:** Image ad / Meta copy / Carousel
- **Internal guardrail:** CTA should stay benefit-led. Use "Check what funding may fit" or "Compare lender matches" without repeating compliance lines in every asset.

### Internal Guardrails for V2

- Keep legal/compliance language in footer, legal notes, or internal review — not repeated in every ad, LP block, VO, and overlay.
- Avoid approval guarantees: never use "guaranteed", "approved", "bad credit accepted".
- Keep the creative benefit-led: educate, make the loan attractive, explain limits, then pivot to lender matching.
- Use natural uncertainty: "may fit", "may suit", "worth checking".
- ✅ For Meta CTAs, prefer safer labels like "Compare options" but include Ian's desired concept in the copy safely
- ❌ Avoid: "See if you qualify" (implies C1 determines eligibility), "Guaranteed funding", "Get approved now"
