# SEO/AEO launch notes — 2026-05-10

## Saved context

Traditional search:

- Sub-pages can rank for long-tail terms like `equipment finance Australia documents needed` or `non-bank business lender comparison`.
- Long-tail terms have lower competition; current content quality is adequate.
- The "funding path before lender" positioning is distinct enough.
- Head terms such as `business loans Australia` and `small business finance` are dominated by Canstar, RateCity, Finder and direct lenders.
- Without backlink profile/domain authority, head-term ranking is a long-term project, not launch-critical.

AI / answer-engine traffic:

- C1 has a better near-term chance here than in head-term traditional search.
- Question-answer structure, differentiation and factual/non-promotional tone match what AI Overviews, Perplexity and ChatGPT Search often cite.
- Missing structured data can reduce extraction/citation eligibility.

Launch technical priorities:

1. Homepage metadata: title tag, meta description, canonical and OG tags.
2. Homepage content depth: expand beyond thin hero copy; add substantive how-it-works and direct Q&A sections.
3. Canonical domain alignment: JSON-LD @id values and page canonicals should use the canonical production alias consistently.
4. FAQ schema on all pages with Q&A/accordion content.
5. WebSite + SearchAction schema on homepage.
6. Individual author bylines with Person schema.
7. External citations on guides: ATO, ASIC, ACCC, business.gov.au, RBA, etc. where relevant.
8. Homepage JSON-LD: WebPage schema matching subpage standards.

## Implementation stance

These are useful, but not all launch blockers. For launch, prioritize technical SEO plumbing that is quick and global: metadata, canonical consistency, JSON-LD, FAQ extraction, Organization/Person schema and homepage WebSite/SearchAction. Deeper content expansion, author bio UX, citations and extra long-tail pages can follow after launch unless a page is obviously thin or legally under-supported.
